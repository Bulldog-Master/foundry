## Why this product exists

Describe the concrete problem this product addresses. Who has this problem, in what situation, and why is it currently unmet or badly met? Avoid slogans and market rhetoric — describe reality.

## Mission

State, in one or two sentences, what this product is committing to do about that problem.

## Users

Who is this product for? Describe the actual people or roles — not personas invented to justify the product. If the user population is uncertain, name that uncertainty rather than hiding it.

## In scope

List, concretely, what this product will do.

## Out of scope

List, concretely, what this product will not do — especially adjacent things it could plausibly be mistaken for.

## Evidence and assumptions

State what is currently known to be true, and separately state what is being assumed. Do not claim market validation that does not yet exist. Naming assumptions honestly is more valuable than dressing them up as facts.

## Success criteria

Describe what "this product is working" would look like — in terms of user-observable outcomes, not internal activity. Include what would cause the founder to reconsider or discontinue the product.

## Constraints

## Risks

The most important ways this product could fail, be wrong, or cause harm — and what is being done, or explicitly not done, about each.

## Constitutional principles this product depends on
