- **Status:** Candidate | Verified
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
- **Author(s):**
- **Source:** (project, incident, ADR, or piece of work this lesson came from)

## Situation

Describe the real situation the lesson emerged from. What was being built, changed, or investigated? What was expected to happen?

## What happened

Describe what actually happened. Include the observable outcome — what worked, what failed, what surprised the people involved. Keep this factual; interpretation belongs in the next section.

## Lesson

State the lesson in a single sentence, then expand as needed. A lesson is a claim about how reality behaves in the domain of the work — not a general opinion.

## Evidence

What evidence supports this lesson? Link to code, logs, measurements, incidents, or repeated observations. A Candidate lesson may be based on a single observation; a Verified lesson requires evidence strong enough that the founder is willing to bind future work to it.

## Consequence

A lesson should normally produce a concrete consequence — a change to a standard, a new ADR, an update to a template, a change in how a product is built, or an explicit decision to do nothing. Record that consequence here, or explain why none is warranted yet.

## Promotion

A lesson is promoted from Candidate to Verified only when:

1. Its evidence has been reviewed;
2. Its consequence has been decided (including an explicit decision to take no action);
3. The founder has explicitly approved promotion.

Promotion is recorded here with date and approver.
